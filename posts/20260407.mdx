I've been listening to Korean learning podcast on my way to work every day. Taking metro gives me a perfect chance to not-procrastinating it.
I used to spent some times in the past to learn Korean, however only through Duolingo. The best I could do at the time was "thank you", "excuse me", and guessing the name of places in bus/metro. This time I went deeper into the language and their cultural aspects, for which I the most curious about while traveling.

I structured my learning around:
- listening to podcasts
- write words in a notebook

By adding contexts around my memory (listening to Korean in a train packed with office workers, writing a foreign language under the yellow light at a coffee shop), I hope the language would stick longer with me.

I told myself that I'm in survival mode, because the fee for my Korean 5-year-entry visa must not be wasted.
